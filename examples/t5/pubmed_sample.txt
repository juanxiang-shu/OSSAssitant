On-Surface Metathesis of an Ionic Liquid on Ag(111).

Massicot S(1), Sasaki T(2), Lexow M(1), Maier F(1), Kuwabata S(2), Steinrück 
HP(1).

Author information:
(1)Lehrstuhl für Physikalische Chemie II, Friedrich-Alexander-Universität 
Erlangen-Nürnberg, Egerlandstr. 3, 91058, Erlangen, Germany.
(2)Department of Applied Chemistry, Graduate School of Engineering, Osaka 
University, Yamada-oka 2-1, Suita, Osaka, 565-0871, Japan.

We investigated the adsorption, surface enrichment, ion exchange, and on-surface 
metathesis of ultrathin mixed IL films on Ag(111). We stepwise deposited 0.5 ML 
of the protic IL diethylmethylammonium trifluoromethanesulfonate ([dema][TfO]) 
and 1.0 ML of the aprotic IL 1-methyl-3-octylimidazolium hexafluorophosphate 
([C8 C1 Im][PF6 ]) at around 90 K. Thereafter, the resulting layered frozen film 
was heated to 550 K, and the thermally induced phenomena were monitored in situ 
by angle-resolved X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy. Between 135 and 200 K, 
[TfO]- anions at the Ag(111) surface are exchanged by [PF6 ]- anions and 
enriched together with [C8 C1 Im]+ cations at the IL/vacuum interface. Upon 
further heating, [dema][PF6 ] and [OMIm][PF6 ] desorb selectively at ∼235 and 
∼380 K, respectively. Hereby, a wetting layer of pure [C8 C1 Im][TfO] is formed 
by on-surface metathesis at the IL/metal interface, which completely desorbs at 
∼480 K. For comparison, ion enrichment at the vacuum/IL interface was also 
studied in macroscopic IL mixtures, where no influence of the solid support is 
expected.
